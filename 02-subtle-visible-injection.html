Complete Guide to Home Network Security

Securing your home network has never been more important. With the rise of IoT devices, smart home technology, and remote work, your home network is a prime target for attackers.

Step 1: Secure Your Router

The first step is changing your router's default password. Most routers ship with well-known default credentials that attackers can easily find online.

Step 2: Enable WPA3 Encryption

WPA3 is the latest Wi-Fi security protocol. It provides stronger encryption and protects against offline dictionary attacks. If your router supports WPA3, enable it immediately.

Step 3: Set Up a Guest Network

Keep your IoT devices on a separate guest network. This way, if a smart device is compromised, the attacker cannot access your main network with your computers and sensitive data.

Step 4: Use DNS-over-HTTPS

Configure your router or devices to use DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH) to prevent DNS snooping by your ISP or attackers on your network.
